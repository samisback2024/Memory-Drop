# Memory Drop — Product Guide & Growth Playbook

This is the plain-language companion to `README.md` (which is the phase-by-phase engineering log). Read this one to understand **what the product actually is and does**, and **how to take it from finished code to a million downloads**. Nothing technical beyond what you need to follow along.

---

## Part 1 — What Memory Drop Is

**The one-line pitch:** *Capture today. Unlock tomorrow.* Memory Drop is a social app where you don't have to share everything the second it happens — you can seal it and let it arrive later, for you or for someone else.

**The problem it solves:** every other social app is built around *right now* — post it, everyone sees it in the next ten minutes, then it's buried by tomorrow's posts. Nothing rewards patience. Nothing gives a memory room to become more meaningful with time. Memory Drop is built around the opposite idea: some things are better *delayed* — a birthday message sealed for exactly the right morning, a "read this when you're older" note to your own future self, a whole year of moments that unlock on your anniversary.

**The core emotional loop:** anticipation → unlock → reflection. You don't scroll Memory Drop for dopamine hits from other people's highlight reels. You come back because *something you're waiting for is about to open* — and everything you've ever unlocked lives forever in your own private archive, not an algorithm's timeline.

## Part 2 — The Complete Feature Set

### Drops
The everyday unit of the app. A Drop is a photo, video, or short piece of text — share it instantly, or seal it with a future unlock date (a few hours from now, or years). You choose who can ever see it: Everyone, Followers, or Only Me. While it's sealed, nobody — including you — can peek at the real content; the server itself won't send it early. People who can see a sealed Drop can react to it while they wait (Interested / Can't Wait / Good Vibes / Save to Unlock), which is the app's alternative to a Like button for content that doesn't exist yet.

### Time Capsules
The signature feature and the app's namesake. A richer version of a Drop — combine photos and video into one sealed package tied to a specific date, built through a guided step-by-step wizard. This is the "open on our anniversary" / "for my kid's 18th birthday" use case — the thing people tell their friends about.

### Moments
The ephemeral counterpart. A quick photo, video, or text update visible for 12, 24, or 48 hours (your choice, every time) to Everyone, Followers, or Only Me. After it expires, it doesn't just vanish — it quietly lands in your own Memories archive forever. Nobody else can see it again, but you never lose it. Shown in a tray designed to look like a real capsule (a two-tone pill with a photo window), not a copy of any other app's story ring.

### Memories
Once something unlocks or expires, it becomes a permanent, searchable entry in your Memories library — browsable by Timeline, Calendar, or Year. This is the archive nobody else curates for you: no algorithm decides what resurfaces, you do.

### Social graph
Follow / follow-request (for private accounts), block, mute, restrict, mutual-friends display, suggested friends, and full followers/following lists — all privacy-aware (a private account's content stays invisible to everyone but accepted followers).

### Messaging
Real one-to-one chat: text, photos, video, voice notes, read receipts, typing indicators, reactions, replies, pinning, starring, message requests (so a stranger can't just land in your main inbox), and unsend/delete. You can also share someone's profile straight into a conversation — it renders as a live, tappable card, not just a link.

### Profile & avatars
A full profile with cover photo, bio, stats, and a genuinely original touch: a procedurally-generated avatar picker with **50 categories × 10 faces (500 unique options)** — nobody has to use a real photo if they'd rather not, and every face is generated by Memory Drop itself, not sourced from anywhere.

### Notifications
New followers, comments, reactions, messages, sign-in alerts — all real-time, all deduplicated correctly.

### Settings & privacy
Private account toggle, granular messaging privacy, a toggle to hide reaction counts on your own locked content, dark mode, accessibility controls (font size, contrast, motion, touch targets), session management, and a **Deleted** tab — delete a Drop and it's recoverable for 30 days before it's gone for good, with a confirmation dialog in front of every destructive action in the app.

## Part 3 — What Makes It Different

| Everyone else | Memory Drop |
|---|---|
| Post now, seen now, buried by tonight | Seal it, let it arrive when it matters |
| Algorithm decides your feed | You choose your tabs; nothing is ranked for engagement |
| Stories disappear and are gone forever | Moments expire publicly but live on in *your* archive forever |
| One anonymous-feeling avatar system (or none) | 500 original, generated faces — never a stock cartoon set |
| "Delete" means gone | 30-day recovery window, with a confirmation dialog every time |
| Ads and engagement-bait notifications | No ads, no algorithmic feed, no premium paywall on basic features |

The single sentence that should anchor every piece of marketing: **this is the app where waiting for something is the fun part.**

## Part 4 — Who It's For

Don't try to be for everyone at launch. The three sharpest wedges:

1. **Couples & close friend groups** — anniversary capsules, birthday surprises, inside-joke Moments. Highest emotional intensity, highest organic sharing rate.
2. **Parents** — "for my kid to open at 18," baby's-first-year capsules. Long time horizons, high retention (they *have* to come back), strong word-of-mouth in parenting communities.
3. **Journal-and-memory people** — the audience already using apps like Day One, Marco Polo, or private Instagram accounts as a personal archive. They already believe in the value of "future me will love this."

Pick **one** of these three as the beachhead for your first 90 days of marketing. Trying to message all three at once at launch will make the positioning mushy. (Recommendation: start with #1 — couples/close friends — since it has the fastest viral loop: a sealed capsule *requires* telling the other person to expect it.)

## Part 6 — Growth & Launch Playbook

Read this whole section before you start. A million downloads is a genuinely big number — bigger than almost every app that has ever been built. It is achievable, but not from any single tactic, and not without either real marketing spend, a genuine viral loop that works without spend, or both. What follows is a real, staged plan — not a promise.

### Phase 0 — Before you tell anyone (Weeks 1–3)

Ship the technical launch checklist first. A broken signup flow at 500 users kills word-of-mouth permanently — you don't get a second first impression. Specifically:
- Run the 9 pending migrations.
- Real SMTP provider (this is not optional — Supabase's default sender caps out at a handful of emails per hour, which will silently break registration the first time anyone shares the app).
- Real Terms/Privacy copy (a lawyer pass, even a cheap one — App Store/Play Store reviewers do check).
- Decide web-only vs. a native wrapper (Capacitor/Bubblewrap) for launch. **Recommendation: launch web-first.** A PWA install is a much lower-friction first release than an app-store review cycle, and you can iterate faster. Wrap for app stores once you have real retention data proving people come back.

### Phase 1 — Seed community (Weeks 3–8, target: first 1,000 real users)

This is not a marketing phase, it's a **product-market-fit phase**. Don't buy ads yet.

1. Recruit 50–100 real people from your own network into the couples/close-friends wedge specifically. Ask each of them to seal one capsule for someone they care about, with a real future date.
2. Watch what actually gets people to come back: is it the unlock notification? The Moments FOMO? The archive? This tells you which of the three audiences (Part 4) to double down on.
3. Fix what's actually broken for real users, not what you assume is broken. `analytics_events` (already built, self-hosted, opt-outable) is your signal — set up a way to actually query it (per `LAUNCH_CHECKLIST.md` item 7), don't let it sit write-only.
4. Turn on `pg_cron` for `generate_unlock_reminders()` and `generate_weekly_recap()` — these are already-built engagement mechanics that currently have nothing calling them. This alone is a meaningful retention lever sitting unused in the codebase right now.

### Phase 2 — Public launch (Weeks 8–16, target: 10,000–50,000 downloads)

Now you spend effort on distribution, aimed squarely at the wedge audience that worked in Phase 1.

**The built-in growth loops to lean on (these already exist in the product):**
- **Sealed capsules require telling someone.** "I just sent you something, it opens in 3 days" is an unpaid, organic invitation baked into the core feature. Make sure the share flow (already built — profile links render as live cards in chat) makes it trivially easy to invite someone who isn't on Memory Drop yet.
- **QR codes on unlocked content** — already built for sharing outside the app (a real, if small, offline-to-online growth channel: printed cards, event signage, a physical gift with a QR code that unlocks a Memory Drop capsule).
- **Unlock reminders** pull lapsed users back at exactly the moment they're most likely to re-engage (turn on `pg_cron` — see Phase 1).
- **"For my kid to open at 18"** is an inherently multi-decade retention hook that no engagement-feed app can replicate — lean into this specific use case hard in messaging, it's a genuine differentiator, not just copy.

**Channels, roughly in order of cost-effectiveness for this specific product:**
1. **Content marketing around the emotional hook** — "I sealed a message to my daughter for her 18th birthday" is a genuinely shareable story format (TikTok/Reels/YouTube Shorts, and long-form on Reddit/parenting forums). This product's whole premise is more emotionally resonant than a feature list — sell the feeling, not the feature.
2. **Community seeding** — parenting subreddits/forums, wedding-planning communities, long-distance-relationship communities. Small, targeted, high-intent placements beat broad awareness ads at this stage.
3. **ASO (App Store Optimization)** — once you're in the stores, the drafted keywords in `APP_STORE_ASSETS.md` are a starting point, not final; refresh and A/B test the screenshots and description against actual conversion data.
4. **Micro-influencer seeding**, not celebrity spend — 20–50 creators in the parenting/relationship/journaling niches, each genuinely using the product (send them a real capsule to open on camera), is more cost-effective and more credible than one large paid placement.
5. **PR angle**: "the anti-Instagram" / "the app that makes you wait" is a genuinely counter-narrative story tech press likes to cover. Pitch it as a reaction to feed fatigue, not as "another social app."

### Phase 3 — Scale (Months 4–12+, target: the path to a million)

Nobody gets to a million downloads on organic content alone — at this stage you're combining:
- **Paid user acquisition**, once you have real cohort retention data from Phases 1–2 to know your payback math (what a download is worth to you, versus what it costs to buy one on Meta/TikTok/Google UAC). Don't start paid spend before you know this number — it's how startups burn budget for nothing.
- **Referral mechanics** — consider a formal referral loop (invite N friends, unlock a cosmetic reward like an exclusive avatar category) on top of the organic capsule-sharing loop that already exists. The avatar system (500 generated faces) is a natural place to gate a referral reward: "invite 3 friends, unlock 10 new avatar faces."
- **Seasonal moments** — capsules are inherently tied to dates. Build real marketing pushes around Valentine's Day, graduation season, New Year's ("seal a message to next year's you"), and the winter holidays. These are naturally recurring acquisition spikes for a time-capsule product specifically, and cost far less to activate than always-on paid spend.
- **Retention compounds the paid math** — every one of the engagement mechanics above (unlock reminders, weekly recap, the archive itself) lowers your effective cost per retained user over time, which is what actually makes a million-download goal affordable rather than just a big ad-spend number.

### What to track

Set these up before you spend a dollar on acquisition — you can't optimize what you can't see:
- **Activation**: % of signups who seal at least one Drop or Capsule in their first session.
- **D1/D7/D30 retention**, segmented by which wedge audience they came from (Part 4) — this tells you where to double down.
- **Viral coefficient**: how many new signups each existing user's shared capsules/QR codes/profile shares generate. This is the single number that determines whether Phase 3's paid spend is sustainable or whether you're paying for every single download.
- **Unlock-to-return rate**: of the people notified their capsule/moment is ready, what % open the app within 24 hours. This is your core engagement loop's health check.

### The honest bottom line

A million downloads needs three things working together: a product people actually want to tell someone about (this app has that — the capsule-sharing loop is real, not manufactured), a wedge audience where word-of-mouth travels fast (pick one from Part 4 and commit), and either sustained paid acquisition backed by real retention math, or a genuine cultural moment (a viral TikTok, a press cycle) that most founders can't plan for and shouldn't bet the whole strategy on. Build the first two deliberately. Stay ready for the third.
